About me

    I am a MS student in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at University of California Berkeley. I also perform student research as part of AutoLab (part of BAIR )in which I work under the supervision of Professor Ken Goldberg. My main research interests are in computer vision - mostly applied to healthcare issues: cancerous cell detection, treatment planning... I also performed theoretical reinforcement learning research.

    Prior to starting my Master, I did a 6-months research scientist internship at Therapanacea, in which I built deep-learning pipelines to predict personalized radiotherapy treatment plans relying solely on patient's MRI information. I then completed a 4-months research scientist internship at Tribun-Healthcare in which I built computer vision models to detect rare mitotic cells among large (10 GB) biopsies digitalized by different scanners. My research was focusing on getting the most accurate predictions while enforcing strong generalization compatibilities on unseen scanners.

    Prior to joining UC Berkeley, I earned an MS in Computer Science from Ecole CentraleSupélec (Paris Saclay) . During that time, I performed research on statistics on a joined project between Servier and CentraleSupélec's MICS lab , where we aim to build a mixed model simulating the action of a diabetes drug, and to perform statistical inference under partial differential equations.
